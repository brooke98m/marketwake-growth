The five structural moves

You cannot get to $50M
from the current structure.
These are the five unlocks.

Everything in the vision exercise, the synthesis document, and the team profiles points to the same five decisions. Each one is a prerequisite for the next. The sequence matters as much as the moves themselves.

01
Transition from channel-based to outcome-based delivery
Retire the SEO, Content, Design, Paid department structure. Launch eight outcome offering teams, each owning a measurable result. This is the single structural change that enables every other move. It changes how you hire, how you sell, how you staff, how you measure, and how clients experience Marketwake. Without this, everything else is cosmetic.
Decide now
02
Build the operational infrastructure that makes the model run
Automated proposals, structured intake, capacity dashboard, billing clarity, KPI infrastructure. Megan's ops build is the precondition for everyone else's vision. Every team described a future where work flows without friction. None of it is possible without the operational foundation underneath it. This is not internal overhead. This is the condition that enables $50M.
Start now
03
Install AI as a baseline standard across every function
Proposals, reports, briefs, audits, client communication, thought leadership — seven documented workflows ready to automate today. This is not a technology project. It is a capacity project. Every hour recovered from manual execution is an hour available for the strategic and creative work the team described wanting to do. AI adoption is the fastest way to increase the quality of output without increasing headcount.
Start now
04
Define a locked ICP and build an intentional sales function
Eight of ten team members described walking away from certain client types as a prerequisite for their vision. Client selection is not a sales problem. It is a strategic lever. The ICP must be company-wide, formally defined, and enforced — not left to individual judgment at the point of sale. Alongside this, build the sales function that actively pursues the right clients, not just any clients. Inbound and outbound working together, both pointed at the same target.
Decide now
05
Build the Marketwake brand as a business development engine
Eight of ten responses described a future where growth comes from inbound — clients who find Marketwake because of its reputation, research, and point of view. Zero described cold outreach as the future model. The thought leadership content program, the internal marketing engine, Josh's AI search frameworks, Kristen's campaign narrative, Bin's internal media vision — these are not nice-to-haves. They are the business development strategy. This requires ownership, a budget, and protected time from leadership.

Our values

Why the values
need to evolve

Our current values — Gritty, Pursuers of Excellence, Solution Seekers, Team Minded, Honest — were written for a different version of this company. They describe what it takes to survive and grind at $5M. They are about effort, attitude, and getting through hard things together. And they worked. They helped us build something real.

But they were written for a smaller firm that competes on hustle. They don't describe how a $50M agency thinks, builds, or wins. Willpower and grit are not a competitive advantage at scale — systems, craft, and compounding intelligence are. The team already knows this. It showed up everywhere in the vision exercise.

What the team described, unprompted
"Radical ownership of revenue impact. Data before opinion. Strategic thinking over platform expertise." — Sammy
"Strategic curiosity. Ownership mentality. Relentless experimentation. Excellence = strategic clarity + creative bravery + measurable growth." — Sammy
"No emotional reactivity. No victim language. Proactive strategy, not reactive reporting. A culture of direct feedback." — Amber
"Taste. Simplicity. Strategic clarity. No wasted work. Creative that converts." — Garrett
"Solve problems once, not repeatedly. If it happens more than a few times, automate it." — Ruben
Current values — what they were built for
Gritty→ pushes through hard things
Pursuers of Excellence→ high standards of effort
Solution Seekers→ figures it out
Team Minded→ collaborative, no silos
Honest→ direct, no politics
Good values for a scrappy, hard-working agency that earns trust through effort. They got us here.
Where they fall short at $50M
Gritty rewards effort over outcomes. At scale we need people building smarter, not just harder.
Pursuers of Excellence is aspirational but vague. It doesn't describe what excellence looks like or how it gets measured.
Solution Seekers is reactive. The future Marketwake builds before the problem exists, not after.
Team Minded is table stakes. Every agency says it. We need a value that describes how we build — not just that we do it together.
